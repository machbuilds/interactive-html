Sample page

A placeholder document you can use to smoke-test the comment library end-to-end. Highlight any prose, click the element picker, or open the floating panel and leave a general note.

How this works. Whatever you point at gets wrapped in a stable anchor and posted to a local server. An agent reads the queue, edits this very HTML in response, and the page reloads with a short tour of what changed.

Three ways to leave a comment

The first is text selection. Drag across any prose and a small inline action appears. Good for "rewrite this", "this is wrong", or "expand on this idea".

The second is element selection. Toggle the picker on, then click a paragraph, image, or table. Hold shift to add more to your selection. Use this when the comment is about a whole region, not a span of text.

The third is page-level. Open the panel, hit "+ general", and leave a free-floating note — useful for "feels too long", "missing a section about X", and other anchorless feedback.
